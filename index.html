The Pitch

Why film?
Why now?

A case for bringing short films into classrooms as tools for social and emotional learning.

"Children don't learn values from lectures. They learn them from stories that make them feel something first."

At KMCP Schools, our students live complex lives. They navigate poverty, caste, climate stress, gender pressure and rapid urbanisation — often without the language to name what they are experiencing. Frames of Change gives them that language.

Every Saturday, 2–3 carefully chosen short films — mostly Indian, mostly in Hindi or regional languages — open a window. Not to preach, but to witness, discuss and question. A 10-minute animated film from Pixar and a 15-minute short from Maharashtra can ask the same question about belonging. A documentary about the Chipko movement and a film by Satyajit Ray can both talk about dignity without wealth.

This is not an add-on. It is core curriculum — for socio-emotional learning, climate citizenship, and civic identity — delivered through the most powerful medium children already love.

The ask

One Saturday morning each week. A projector. A teacher who listens more than they speak. And students who are trusted to think for themselves.

🎞️

Short films democratise emotion

A 7-minute Pixar short does what a 90-minute lecture cannot — it makes students feel before they think, which is the only order that creates lasting change.

🇮🇳

Indian stories, Indian contexts

80% of films are in Hindi, Bengali, Tamil or Marathi. Students see their own world — kite-flying, village wells, school tiffins — on screen. Representation builds trust.

🗣️

Student-led discussion panels

Every session ends with a structured panel where students take roles — Questioner, Devil's Advocate, Connector, Summariser. The teacher observes. The students lead.

🎬

Students become filmmakers

The year ends with students making and screening their own 2–5 minute short films. They don't just consume stories — they create them. At the Year-End Film Festival, they become the directors.

🌱

Continuity across the year

Films from Week 2 are revisited in Week 17. A seedling planted in Week 6 is brought to the festival in Week 18. The curriculum builds memory, not just knowledge.
